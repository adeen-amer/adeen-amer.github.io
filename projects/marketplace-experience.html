Marketplace seller experience

At Dubizzle Labs (EMPG), I contributed to products used by thousands of sellers. This write-up focuses on how we reduced ambiguity in listing and post-sale flows.

Context
High-traffic classifieds and vertical marketplaces (OLX Seller Center, Zameen.com, Dubizzle Cars).
My role
Product manager — discovery, prioritisation, specs and launch support with engineering and design.
Methods
User feedback loops, competitive scans, PRDs, stakeholder workshops, release readouts.

Problem statement

Sellers abandoned flows when shipping rules, fees or verification were explained too late. Trust erodes when the product feels clever instead of transparent — especially where money and reputation move together.

Situation & task

Situation: High-traffic classifieds and verticals with different policy packs per category. Task: Improve completion on listing and post-sale steps without slowing compliant sellers down.

Research & insights

  • Qualitative feedback and support themes showed “surprise fees” and document gaps as top drop-off drivers.
  • Competitive scans highlighted where peers front-loaded trust signals; we borrowed patterns that fit our policy model.

Design decisions

I pushed for progressive validation (errors in context) instead of a final “error wall” because sellers fix mistakes faster when the field still matters. I chose plainer language over internal jargon even when legal review lengthened copy cycles — clarity won.

Launch & measurement

Every meaningful step shipped with analytics names, support macros and a rollback lever. Post-launch readouts paired funnel deltas with a short qualitative note from support leads.

Challenges

Policy varies by category and region. I templated decision trees so legal and ops could review once, then reuse across squads.

Impact

Where instrumentation existed, we saw completion uplift on revised steps (figures confidential). Support volume on “confusing fees” trended down after copy and layout iterations — a directional signal we treated as seriously as percentage points.
