Every metric needs a story

15 March 2026

Dashboards fail when nobody can act on them. A chart without a decision is wallpaper. I treat every metric as a contract between data and leadership: what we measure, who owns the response and how often we review it.

The three lines I add next to every chart

  1. Decision: What changes if this number moves up or down?
  2. Owner: Who has authority to act in the next sprint — not “the team,” a name.
  3. Cadence: Weekly, monthly or milestone-bound; “when someone asks” is not a cadence.

Why vanity metrics creep in

They are easy to instrument and politically safe. I push back by asking for the counterfactual: if we ignore this metric for a month, what breaks? If the answer is “nothing,” we delete it from the executive view.

Takeaway

If a number cannot change how we spend the next sprint, it is vanity, not signal. Stories keep metrics honest.
