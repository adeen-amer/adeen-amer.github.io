# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Recruiters, hiring managers, founders, and potential collaborators evaluating Adeen Amer as a product manager. They arrive skeptical and time-pressed — often mid-shortlist, scanning many candidates — and want to answer one question fast: *can this person own a product end-to-end and ship?* Context is usually a desk browser during a hiring sprint, sometimes mobile from a LinkedIn or email link. The job to be done: get enough signal (judgment, range, evidence of shipped work) to decide whether to reach out.

## Product Purpose

A personal portfolio that argues Adeen is a product manager who ships — not a slide-deck PM. It exists to convert a cold visitor into a conversation by showing real, decision-driven case studies (ChordLift built and deployed to production, marketplace seller work at Dubizzle/EMPG, data-to-decisions, healthcare UX) backed by a verifiable CV. Success = a qualified visitor reads at least one case study and reaches the contact form (or emails / connects on LinkedIn).

## Brand Personality

Calm, technically precise, quietly bold. Voice is plain and declarative — "I build calm software for noisy markets" — confident without hype. It states decisions and trade-offs directly, prefers evidence to adjectives, and never pads. The boldness shows up as clarity and conviction (a strong thesis, decisive layout, a live product link), not as volume, neon, or motion. Emotional goal: a hiring manager should feel *trust* and *competence* within seconds, and mild surprise that a PM's site is this well-built.

## Anti-references

This should NOT look like:
- **Flashy gradient SaaS** — gradient hero text, glassmorphism, the big-number hero-metric template, neon accents. (The current monochrome, flat, shadowless system is a deliberate rejection of this.)
- **Generic template portfolio** — the default theme/Bootstrap portfolio with stock photography, identical icon-heading-text card grids, and a "Hi, I'm X" pattern.
- **Corporate & stiff** — stock-photo professionalism and soulless enterprise tone.
- **Over-designed & loud** — heavy animation, decorative effects, and visual noise that competes with the work itself.

## Design Principles

1. **Show, don't tell.** Lead with shipped evidence — a live product URL, real outcomes, named trade-offs — not with adjectives about being "passionate" or "results-driven."
2. **Calm confidence, not volume.** Boldness comes from a sharp thesis, decisive hierarchy, and restraint held with intent — never from loudness. Quiet but never timid.
3. **The site is the proof.** Build quality (performance, accessibility, responsiveness, polish) is itself an argument for the candidate. Practice what you preach.
4. **Every project is an argument.** Case studies follow problem → role → decisions/trade-offs → outcome, so the site reads like a body of reasoning, not a gallery of screenshots.
5. **Respect the reader's time.** Fast scan paths, clear next steps, no dark patterns or padding. The visitor is busy and skeptical; reward that.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target: push toward **WCAG 2.1 AAA** where feasible, with the existing AA posture as the hard floor (documented in `docs/ACCESSIBILITY.md`). Maintain: visible focus states, skip-to-content links, semantic landmarks, labeled forms with live-region validation, and full `prefers-reduced-motion` alternatives. Toward AAA: aim for 7:1 contrast on body text and 4.5:1 on large text; keep tap targets ≥44px; ensure the site is fully usable keyboard-only and at 200% zoom. Accessibility is treated as a craft signal, not a compliance checkbox.
