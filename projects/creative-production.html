Case study · Creative

Creative production

Music in FL Studio, motion in Premiere and After Effects, and live experiences for 1000+ guests — the through-line is pacing and emotional clarity.

Formats
Audio releases, branded video, recap films, stage programming.
My role
Producer, editor and event lead (Music Society of LUMS — External Affairs).
Principles
Respect the audience’s time; design dynamics before effects.

Problem statement

Creative portfolios often show outputs without process. I document how pacing, constraints and audience empathy shaped the final cut — the same narrative rigour I bring to product work.

Live events

Situation: LUMS Music Fest and society programming with 1000+ guests. Task: Coordinate artists, safety and run-of-show without losing humanity. Action: Clear riders, volunteer roles and contingency slots for technical drift. Result: Artists trusted the timeline; I learned production is logistics plus empathy.

Studio work

In the box, I iterate with reference tracks and ruthless arrangement cuts so hooks land early for social formats. I choose dynamics before plug-ins — if the mute button does not move someone, no preset will save it.

Challenges

Creative work rarely has one KPI. I pair qualitative feedback (crowd energy, peer critique) with lightweight analytics when platforms allow — retention on cuts, completion on recaps.
